The P&L Bleed Every CFO Needs to See

"Most practices are hemorrhaging $500K-$1.5M annually on pre-encounter prep costs—hitting three different expense lines."

Medical Record Retrieval Costs

$250K-500K/year

Manual medical record retrieval costs $150+ per patient in most states. For a practice handling 2,000+ retrievals annually, that's $300K-500K hitting your expense line. Search fees, prep fees, per-page charges—it's death by a thousand cuts.

For a 10-provider practice: $300K-500K annual spend on something that should be automated. That's 3-5x your entire Credo subscription cost.

Pre-Encounter Prep Labor Waste

$150K-300K/year

Clinical staff spend 5-10 minutes per patient manually assembling charts, reviewing records, and flagging issues. At loaded costs of $30-50/hour, that adds up fast. $50/day per provider × 10 providers × 250 days = $125K annually in pure labor waste.

Manual pre-encounter prep consumes $150K-300K in annual labor costs. Money that could go toward patient care, quality initiatives, or straight to your bottom line.

Missed Revenue Capture

$200K-500K/year

When providers walk into appointments without complete context, they code conservatively (Level 3 vs Level 4 = $50-60 lost per visit), miss HCC diagnoses ($3K-8K lost per patient annually), and leave care gaps open (HEDIS/Star penalties).

For a 10-provider practice, incomplete pre-encounter prep leaves $200K-500K on the table every year. Revenue you've already earned but can't capture.
